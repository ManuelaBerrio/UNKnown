The Issue

Immigrants make up 14.2% of the US population. However, their quality of life in the States differs starkly from US-born people.

Immigrants and their children make up 26% of the US population. By 2065, they are projected to be 36%. Despite the large population, they are underrepresented in education, occupations, and more. Often, immigrants feel as if they’re not prepared to navigate socially through a foreign country. Through a source where they can easily access information about such topics, we hope to decrease the disparity between immigrants and the US-born population.

Data

Visualizing the Disparities



Education Rates

This data from the Pew Research Center displays that immigrants are more likely to end with a lower level of education, shown with a higher rate of immigrants with a lower level of education, and a lower rate of immigrants with a higher level of education.





Poverty Rates

From the Pew Research Center, the data shows that in each age group, immigrants have a higher rate of people in poverty. This leads to many disparities in other aspects of life, such as education.





English Proficiency Rates

The Pew Research Center data portrays the English proficiency rates for immigrants in the US. The rates have been around 50%-60% between years 1980 and 2018, which is very low and could make life in the US very difficult.
